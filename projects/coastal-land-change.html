Coastal Land Change — North Jakarta

Seven years of satellite imagery, one question: how much of North Jakarta's coastline has actually changed since 2019 — and where is it changing fastest?

Google Earth Engine Python Sentinel-2 Geemap
Live Demo GitHub Repository

The challenge

Business context

Coastal authorities and urban planners need consistent, multi-year visibility into land reclamation and coastal development to manage flood risk and evaluate permit compliance — but manual site surveys can't keep pace with how quickly reclamation actually happens. This project used free, repeat satellite imagery to build that visibility instead.

Questions I asked

🌊

How much land has been reclaimed since 2019?

📉

Where is water disappearing fastest?

🏗️

Does bare-soil growth predict where construction follows?

01 / FEATURE ENGINEERING

Turning raw imagery into a water mask

I computed AWEI_sh (Automated Water Extraction Index) from Sentinel-2 bands to reliably separate water from land across cloud-masked composites, then tracked total water area year over year.

💡 Water area declined steadily nearly every year since 2019 — not a single dramatic event, but a slow, compounding shift.

02 / EDA JOURNEY

Where is reclamation actually happening?

Using ENDBSI (Enhanced Normalized Difference Bare Soil Index) to flag newly-bare land, I broke down bare-soil growth by coastal zone to find where reclamation was concentrated rather than spread evenly.

💡 One zone accounted for nearly 40% of all new bare-soil area — a clear priority target for monitoring and permit review.

The conclusion, not the beginning

Bringing it together

A combined view of water loss, bare-soil growth, and land-cover mix gives planners a single reference point instead of stitching together separate satellite passes by hand.

Key takeaways

What the data actually said

🌊

Water area has shrunk steadily since 2019 — a gradual trend, not a single reclamation event.

📍

Reclamation concentrated heavily in one zone, accounting for nearly 40% of all new bare-soil area.

🏗️

Bare-soil expansion tended to precede visible construction by roughly a year — a useful early signal.

So what should change

Business recommendations

01

Prioritize the highest-change zone for compliance review, since it accounts for a disproportionate share of new reclaimed land.

02

Automate this pipeline to run quarterly in Google Earth Engine rather than as a one-off analysis, so change is caught as it happens.

03

Cross-reference bare-soil growth against the permit database — since it tends to precede construction, it can double as an early compliance flag.

Reflection

Cloud cover was the most persistent technical obstacle — building a solid cloud-masking step early on saved every analysis downstream from noisy false positives. If I extended this, I'd bring in official permit records to validate whether bare-soil growth really does predict construction, rather than relying on visual inspection alone.
